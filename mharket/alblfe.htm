Morten Harket - Letter From Egypt (19.05.08)

Morten's second English sung solo album "Letter From Egypt" was released in Norway on 19 May 2008. The cover features a shot of Morten in Morocco, standing just outside a circle of rocks which have red religious symbols painted on them. In the background there is a girl standing by a deserted telephone box and a car on a distant track. Above the picture, "morten harket" is included in white hand written letters, with the title "LETTER FROM EGYPT" typed in black typewriter font underneath. The cover gives the effect to look like it has a big crease down the centre and along the bottom of the sleeve.

The album is available in both a digi-pak edition and in a jewel case in Norway, but include a different Catalogue number on each one. Both have the same album cover design and come with an eight page "dirty looking and coffee stained" booklet featuring the lyrics and track information. It also includes some album credits at the end. The booklet, which comes with the digi-pak has been printed in slightly darker tones. Along the bottom of each lyric page is some grey hand-written quotes, which read: "As I was walking down the street last night". "The streets were no longer mine". "I was heading for the park". "A dog strayed in front of me". "It was the night of the sleepless people". "Their clouds drifting above the treetops". "But it was the youth in the park that really owned the night". "Exuberant amidst the flickering shadows". "None of this, however, belonged to me, yet still I was included". "A door here and there slammed to and the stars started to appear". "It was the end of August". "In this troubled, angelic town". "Which you don’t leave before you receive its blessings". The discs are black with white titles and text. "morten harket" is again displayed handwritten below the centre ring with the album title "LETTER FROM EGYPT" in typewriter font underneath. There is some record company production and copyright information included: "℗ Swerve © 2008 Universal Music GmbH Germany / Universal Music AS, Norway. All rights of the producer and of the owner of the work reserved. Unauthorised copying, hiring, lending, public performance and broadcasting of this recording is prohibited! All rights reserved". The Catalogue number for the digipak edition Cat. No: 0602517732674 – LC 00309 and the jewel case edition Cat. No: 0602517732650 – LC 00309 are also located at the bottom of each disc, as is the "UNIVERSAL MUSIC GROUP" and "Polydor" logos. On the back cover, it includes a picture of a deserted telephone box in Morocco, printed on the same folded dirty looking paper. The full track listing (shown in grey) is located at the bottom left-hand side, with the same "UNIVERSAL MUSIC GROUP" and "polydor" logos, and record company production and copyright information, which appears on the discs, printed in black on the right-hand side. The Catalogue numbers for each edition is also included in addition to a barcode containing the same relevant number on each one. The inside of the digi-pak is plain and features more of the dirty folded paper and no further artworks. The booklet housed within the casing on the left hand side of the digi-pak and the disc held in a plastic transparent tray on the right.

Tracks: Darkspace / Send Me An Angel / We'll Never Speak Again / There Are Many Ways To Die / With You - With Me / Letter From Egypt / A Name Is A Name / Movies / Shooting Star / Anyone / Should The Rain Fall / The One You Are

All tracks are written by Morten Harket and/or Ole Sverre Olsen with music by Morten and/or Kjetil Bjerkestrand, except "Send Me An Angel" written by Ole Sverre Olsen and Tanya; "We'll Never Speak Again" written by Magne Furuholmen with music by Magne and Kjetil Bjerkestrand and "Movies" written by Kåre Vestrheim, Georg Buljo, Hans Jørgen Støp, Thomas Tofte and Anders Engen. The album was produced and arranged by Kjetil Bjerkestrand and co-produced by Morten. Musicians on the album include Morten Harket (vocals and guitar), Kjetil Bjerkestrand (keyboards), Per Lindvall (drums), Jenny Sjo (bass), Frode Alnæs (guitar), Eivind Aarset (guitar), Kjetil Steensnæs (guitar), Gunnar Andreas Berg (guitar), Petur Jonsson (guitar), Helge Norbakken (percussion), Ashild Breie Nyhus (viola), Magne Furuholmen (additional vocals - track 3)

There is a Norwegian 4-track promo album sampler. The disc is white with "Fire spor fra Morten Harket Letter from Egypt Som kommer 19. Mai" printed at the top of the disc and the four tracks: We'll Never Speak Again / Send Me An Angel / With You - With Me / A Name Is A Name" printed at the bottom. The Polydor and Universal logos are printed on the right hand and left hand side of the disc respectively. It doesn't come with a sleeve.
