True North (21.10.22)

a-ha released their album "True North" on 21 October. The album was recorded during a week at Sv&oslsh;mmehallen Scene concert hall in Bodø, Norway at the end of October/beginning of November 2021. The album features the Arctic Philharmonic orchestra. a-ha were joined by Even Ormestad, Kjetil Bjerkestran, Karl Oluf Wennerberg and Per Hillestad. The album has 12 tracks and was released on several different formats: CD, special edition, double LP black vinyl and a special recycled double LP coloured vinyl unique to the UK, France and Germany.

The cover art is white with a shot of the sea and rocky coastline in the north of Norway. "a-ha" is printed in silver above with "True North" below. The reverse is also white with another shot from nortern Norway, this time looking out towards the mountains over the sea with some white house in the foreground. The tracks are listed in silver in the centre of the sleeve, partially over the picture. Track and copyright information is printed below in black.

There are two different versions of the double LP set which comes in identical packaging, the only difference is that the special recycled coloured edition has a sticker over the barcode on the reverse of the sleeve with a different catalogue number. The LPs come in light grey papaer sleeve with the lyrics printed in white.

In the regular edition, the LPs are black with grey labels with a white compass in the centre. Each disc has one of "North", "East", "South" or "West" printed in white at the appropriate point on the compass, with the other compass points only having "N","E", "S" or "W". The "a-ha" logo is printed in white at the top with "True North", "Sony Music" and "RCA" in white at the bottom. "45 r.p.m." is printed in white on the right hand side of the label, except for on the "East" disc where it is printed on the left.

 

The cover of the album is white with a shot of, presumably the arctic, sea breaking on to the beach in the middle. Above is the a-ha logo in grey and the title below.
