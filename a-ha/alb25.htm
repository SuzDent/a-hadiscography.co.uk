25 (19.07.2010 / 04.10.2010)


A new "best of" album "25" was released in Norway on 19 July and in the UK on 4 October. It was also released in Austria, Czech Republic, Germany, Hungary, Poland and Slovakia (6 August); Japan and Spain (27 September?); Australia, Belgium, Brazil, Canada, Eastern Europe, Finland, France, Greece, Ireland, Italy, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Portugal and Sweden (4 October) It is a 2 disc set featuring 39 tracks spanning the 25 years of a-ha. The final track on disc 2 is the last single "Butterfly, Butterfly (The Last Hurrah)". The album also features the "DJ Edit" of "Tounchy!" and an edit of "I Call Your Name" (4:29). It is worth noting that unless specified, album versions of the tracks have been included, even where a single edit exists (TSASOTV, SMO, Velvet, FNY, Lifelines, Celice, FOTM).

The front cover features a shot of a beach with two large rocks in the foreground. The new a-ha logo in white plus a large "25" in similar font but filled with an image of a cloudy sky are placed on the horizon against the sky. The back cover features another seaside shot with a lonely rock sitting in the centre of a pool. The track listings are printed in white down the middle. The Norwegian version comes with a white circular sticker on the front with the following text: SE A-HA LIVE FOR SISTE GANG 21/8 Oslo, 28/8 Bergen, 2/9 Stavanger, 3/9 Tromso, 4/9 Trondheim, 11/9 Kristiansand.

The CD comes with a very glossy 8 page double sided booklet with grey-blue arty shots plus English text about the band's career and track information. The discs are silver with a large white "25" across the middle and a-ha logo in white on the left hand side. The centre of disc one has a grey ring where as disc two has a bright blue ring in the centre.

The UK edition is identical to the Norwegian edition except for the sticker which comes on the front. The sticker on the Norwegian release is circular, white with blue and black text. The sticker on the UK edition is rectagular, blue with white text.

Some copies of the album were used for promotional purposes and came additionally with an orange "Hesso Media" sticker on the reverse of the case showing some history information and record company contact details which reads; a-ha - '25 - The Very Best Of (Album) Released 4th October 2010. To mark this monumental final year for the iconic pop band. Rhino are set to release the ultimate collection from a-ha's 25-year career. Furthermore, exclusive to the release comes a-ha's last ever recording and brand new single 'Butterly, Butterfly (the Last Hurrah)'. This career retrospective release is packed full with 39 tracks from a-ha's great plethora of music. Uniquely separated into chronological order and distinguished by the band's separate album releases, the two-disc CD plays homage to all of a-ha's greatest tracks including 'Take on Me', 'The Sun Always Shines On T.V.', 'The Living Daylights' and 'Foot of the Mountain'. After nine albums and sales of more than 36 million worldwide, it was announced that a-ha will embark on a farewell tour (including the Royal Albert Hall on Friday 8 October) and that 2010 will mark the end to the expansive career of the pop greats. Variations

Argentina: The CD and Deluxe Editions were both released in Argentina. They are similar to the EU release but have a silver "ifpi" hologram sicker on the bottom front right hand corner and state that they were made by Warner Music Argentina.

Brazil: The Brazilian CD album is similar to the European pressing but the blue sticker on the front has text in Portuguese and it was manufactured by Warner Music Brazil Ltda. The back sleeve includes an additional compact disc logo plus flying Amazon bird logo and Brazilian website addresses. The code "AA0002000" on the spine suggests that 2000 copies of the album were pressed in Brazil.

Chile: The deluxe 2CD + 1DVD edition is similar to the UK deluxe edition but has additional "Musica & Marketing Chile Ltda", "compact disc", "NTSC ALL REGIONS" and "DVD Video" logos on the back sleeve. There is also Chile marketing and manufacture information on the back cover. The sticker on the front is present but printed slightly darker than the UK edition and there is also a silver ifpi hologram sticker.

India: The Indian release is very similar to the European release but has a silver "IMI" hologram sticker on the front. The back sleeve includes some marketing, manufacture and copyright India text

Indonesia: The Indonesian edition is similar to the European version, but the artwork is less shiny with more of a matt feel and on stiffer paper. There is a silver hologram sticker with "WARNER MUSIC INDONESIA" on the front of the booklet. There is also an orange and yellow "Warner music" sticker on the cover. The discs also has a matt feel, rather than the usual shine and have Indonesia manufacturing informationon them, as doe the back cover.

Japan: The Japanese edition comes with an obi-strip and the track list differs. The front and spine of the obi strip are blue with white, yellow and light blue text. There is a picture of the band from the early years at the top on the front of the obi. The reverse of the obi has a similar background as the back sleeve of the album cover withe the tracks printed in white. Small pictures of the "Hunting High And Low" and "Scoundrel Days" covers are printed at the bottom on a white background with text in black. There is also a white 40 anniversary Warner Music Japan sticker on the front of the CD.

 

The Japanese edition has two less tracks than the European release and the tracks are in a completely different order. "There's Never A Forever Thing", "Early Morning", "Cold As Stone", "The Sun Never Shone That Day", "Forever Not Yours", "Did Anyone Approach You?", "Celice", "Cosy Prisons" and "Nothing Is Keeping You Here" have been replaced with "Rolling Thunder", "And You Tell Me", "Living A Boy's Adventure Tale", "Out Of Blue Comes Green", "Waiting For Her", "Here I Stand And Face The Rain" and "The Weight Of The Wind". The album comess in a slimline double jewelcase. It comes with the regular CD booklet which is similar to the European release but with the track list altered accordingly. It also comes with a 45 page white booklet which includes all the lyrics in both English and Japanese.

The promo edition is the same as the regular edition but has a white promo sticker with red text over the barcode on the back of the obi and "SAMPLE - LOADNED" etched in orange letters around the centre of the discs.
