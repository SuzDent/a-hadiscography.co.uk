East Of The Sun West Of The Moon (22.10.90)

The front of the CD insert features a black and white group photo. Inside the booklet are 12 black and white pictures plus song lyrics. The back insert of the CD is black with grey "a-ha" writing, purple rectangle and silver titles. The CD is silver with purple titles and "a-ha" logo (in similar font to that on the front sleeve). The LP has a black inner sleeve with 3 individual pictures and lyrics. The album is produced by Chris Neil, except for * by Ian Stanley.

The German made cassette has cover similar to the LP and CD. The insert folds out with the lyrics printed in black on a white background. There are also three black and white individual shots of the band members plus further information in white and yellow text on a purple background. The spine of the insert is white with black titles. The actual cassette is transparent / grey with white titles. The UK cassette comes with a white circular sticker with black text "Includes CRYING IN THE RAIN and I CALL YOUR NAME 7599-26314-4 WX378C".

There were at least two different pressings of the CD available in Germany. Both have a similar sleeve design, but the actual discs differ. The first has the a-ha logo printed using the same font as that on the front sleeve. The second version has the a-ha logo printed in plain purple capital letters. Both versions have the same catalogue number.

Tracks: Crying In The Rain (4:25) / Early Morning* (2:59) / I Call Your Name (4:54) / Slender Frame (3:42) / East Of The Sun* (4:47) / Sycamore Leaves (5:22) / Waiting For Her* (4:49) / Cold River (4:40) / The Way We Talk* (1:30) / Rolling Thunder (5:43) / (Seemingly) Non-stop July* (2:55).

Best In Brazil (1991) - approx. 350,000 copies sold

This Brazilian only release has a colour photo cover. The band shot (Pål, Morten, Mags) is placed on the right hand side of the sleeve, filling approximately 3/4 of the cover. The remainding quarter is yellow. The title in blue with pink trim is placed in the top left corner.

The reverse of the LP is lemon coloured with blue / grey writing and a picture of a-ha in a field. There is also an inner sleeve with three individual large pictures. The reverse of the inner sleeve is yellow with blue / grey a-ha-logo and four pictures of the previous albums.

The CD insert folds out. Inside the insert there is a black and white group photo of the band standing in a field. On the reverse there are three individual colour pictures. The back insert has the colour version of the picture found inside the CD insert on a lemon coloured background. There is also a blue and pink edged "a-ha" logo plus blue titles.

Headlines And Deadlines The Hits Of a-ha (04.11.91)

This compilation album of a-ha hits "Headlines And Deadlines" features 16 tracks including the song "Move To Memphis" which was recorded especially to be included on the album.

The cover of the LP features a colour group-shot (Morten, Magne and PÂl) by Just Loomis. The a-ha logo is printed in beige in the bottom right corner with the title in blue and white underneath. The reverse is black with an a-ha picture in the corner as on the "Move To Memphis" single.

The CD has similar cover to the LP. The 6 page CD booklet features pictures used on the packaging of the different single releases on a black background. Further all text is written in beige, white and blue lettering. The reverse of the CD booklet and back insert feature a black and white with green and pink tinge group-shot (as found on the "Move To Memphis" single cover). The back insert also features a beige track listing and other white text.
The actual CD is navy blue with titles and a-ha-logo in beige.

Note: Throughout Europe, the disc colour varies, with some being more green/blue in colour.
The German made cassette has cover similar to the LP and CD. The sleeve fold out. One side has a black background with seven black and white band shots and white text. The other side also has a black background but there are ten group shots plus text in blue, white and beige. The spine of the sleeve is black with blue, white and beige text. The actual cassette is transparent / grey with white titles.

There was a "Tape Tape" promo cassette produced for "Headlines And Deadline". It comes in a white paper sleeve with the tracks printed in typewriter-face on the front. It is labelled as "The Best of a-ha". Two tracks are missing compared to the regular release "Early Morning" and "Train Of Thought (Remix)". The cassette itself is a regular audio cassette with typed on sticker along the top.

In December 2002, "Headlines And Deadlines" was re-released in the UK. It has the same catalogue number as previous, but comes in a special card slip case featuring a recent band shot (Paul, Morten, Magne).

Headlines And Deadlines was also released inside a special picture card slip case. More details to follow..

Holland: This album was also released on DCC (Digital Compact Cassette) which is made in Holland. The design of the cover and booklet are similar to the design of the regular album and the spine is very similar to the video release.

Indonesia: The Indonesian cassette is similar to the European release with fold out sleeve but has the text "Hanya Untuk Dijual Di Indonesia" printed in white at the top of the sleeve. It also has the catalogue numbers printed in white above the bar code on the back flap.

Japan: The Japanese CD album comes with a white and green obi strip with red, black, yellow, blue and white text. It comes with a white folded lyric sheet with tracks in both English and Japanese. The promo edition has a white and red promo sticker on the reverse plus the disc has some black "promo" text printed in black around the centre of the disc.

 


Headlines And Deadlines was re-released in Japan as a "Hot Price" album. It has a different catalogue number compared to the "regular" release. The obi strip is white with green text and the red and yellow "bomb" hot price logo.

There is another Japanese re-issue which was released after the "Lifelines" album. It comes with a black obis strip with gold, blue and white text plus a colour shot of the "Lifelines" album. The disc comes in a white disc tray and is a different shade of blue compared with the EU release and the a-ha logo is printed in light pink. It comes with the same booklet as the EU release, but with deeper blue text and a different catalogue number. It also comes with a white paper booklet with the lyrics in both Japanese and English. The catalogue number is WPCR-1088.

The album was re-released on 10 October 2007 and is similar to the release WPCR-1088 but has a different obi-strip and catalogue number. The obi-strip is white with red and black text plus the price (1,500 yen) printed in a red circle at the top. The catalogue number is WPCR-13055.

The Japanese album was re-issued again on 1 April 2009, it is almost identical to the WPCR-1088 release described above but has a different obi-strip and catalogue number. The obi-strip is white with black and red text plus a large red "special price" circle with the price printed in yellow. The back of the obi-strip is white with a "Warner Music Life" advert. It also comes with the white paper lyric booklet. The catalogue number is WPCR-13393.

Malaysia: The Malaysian cassette differs slightly compared to the European release. All of the text on the sleeve is white rather than some of it being in tan and light blue. Although this release has the same catalogue number as the European release, the additional numbers WX450C and WE491 are no present. The cassette itself is transparent/grey with white text and includes an edited catalogue number 26773-4 plus the Warner logo.

Mexico: The cassette differs slightly from the European release, the sleeve does not fold out and the text on the cover and spine is printed in white. The track listing is printed in Spanish on the sleeve and on the cassette itself.

Norway: The German made CD released in Norway has a slightly different catalogue number to that released in the UK.

Peru: The release from Peru looks unofficial with the regular cover artwork printed in colour in the middle of the front sleeve which is otherwise plain white with black type-font text. The title is printed in red on the spine. The tracks are listed on the fold-out part in Spanish and English. The cassette is transparent with white paper labels with black text plus the WB logo.

The Philippines: The Philippines CD release of "Headlines And Deadlines" differs slightly from the European release. The front and back sleeves are similar to the European release, except for the text in the right hand corner of the front sleeve is printed in white rather in three colours and all text on the back sleeve is in white. Also, all of the internal booklet text is also printed in white. The actual disc is black with a large orange a-ha logo at the top. The remainder of the text is printed in silver, set out in a similar way to that on the European release. The CD was manufactured and distributed by WEA Music Philippines. The cassette release also has the text all printed in black and white. The cassette itself is transparent grey with light blue printed labels.

Russia: In Russia, the album was released as a set of two LP's. They come in individual sleeves which have covers similar to the regular European release of "Headlines And Deadlines" except for a circled 1 printed on the sleeve of the first LP and a 2 on the other.

 

The reverse of the sleeves are also similar to that of the regular release but the titles are listed in a slightly different manner. The centre of the records have a pinkish red paper label which says "The Hits Of a-ha Headlines And Deadlines stereo 33 Volume 1 (/ 2)" The running order of the LPs are the same as the CD edition with "Manhattan Skyline" starting the second side of LP1, "Move To Memphis" starting LP2 and "I Call Your Name" starting the second side of LP2. The LP-set was released in October 1992.
There was a Russian made cassette release of "Headlines & Deadlines" available in Russia. It has a similar sleeve to the European release, but without fold-out insert. The cassette is transparent with white text. It wasn't orignally released on cassette in Russia, but was available on cassette at a later date.
"Headlines And Deadlines" was re-issued in a card slipcase in 2003, click here for more details.

South Africa: The South African release of "Headlines And Deadlines" differs slightly from the European release. The front and back sleeves are similar to the European release, except for but the text in the right hand corner of the front sleeve is printed in white rather in three colours and all text on the back sleeve is in white. The actual disc is silver with a large orange a-ha logo at the top. The remainder of the text is printed in black, set out in a similar way to that on the European release. The CD was manufactured by CDT in South Africa.

 

Taiwan: There is a Taiwan Release of "Headlines and Deadlines" which comes with a red and yellow fire design obi-strip which covers the whole of the back of the case. The obi includes coloured text, blue stars with "The very best" in yellow plus the track listing. The disc itself is dark green with white text. It comes with the usual insert booklet, but all of the text is in white rather than a combination of white and colours, as is the title on the front sleeve.

This release also comes with a large double-sided white folded paper sheet which has the lyrics in English, some other Taiwanese text about the band, plus three black and white shots of a-ha. Although the catalogue number is the same as the EU release, the back cover indicates that the CD was made in Taiwan by UFO Group under licence from WEA International inc.
